2) Recruitment, Onboarding & Training Doctrine

Our HRM doctrine is built on a foundation of dynastic continuity and operational modernity. We are not just hiring staff; we are cultivating the future guardians of the Alo Chaya legacy. Our core mantra: "We are hosts, not staff."

A. Recruitment Philosophy: The Local First Initiative

  • Community Integration: We are deeply committed to prioritizing hiring from the local Chandpur community. This is a core business strategy to create sustainable employment, foster strong local ties, and ensure our team has an authentic connection to the land and its heritage.
  • Targeted Expertise: For highly specialized roles (e.g., Sous Chef Trainer), we will conduct targeted recruitment to bring in best-in-class expertise that can be used to train and elevate our local team.

B. Onboarding: The Alo Chaya Immersion

Every new team member will undergo a mandatory 7-day "Alo Chaya Immersion" program personally led by you. This is a cultural and spiritual induction into our world, covering family heritage, our wellness philosophy, and our core operational standards.

C. Training Doctrine (Shokugeki-Style): The House of Light Academy

Philosophy: Excellence is earned, not assigned. Every team member, from the kitchen "Gastronauts" to the "Office Versatilists," must prove their skill, creativity, and commitment through structured, meritocratic challenges under direct mentorship.

  • Challenge-Based Learning: Weekly culinary and operational challenges ("battles") that test problem-solving, improvisation, and innovation under pressure, with real-time feedback from you and senior mentors.
  • Mentor-Mentee Ladder: Every junior member is paired with a senior mentor who tracks their growth and unlocks new responsibilities based on demonstrated mastery in challenges, not just tenure.
  • Gamified Recognition: Monthly showcase events and internal competitions where winners earn tangible rewards like badges, privileges, or first-choice shifts, fostering a culture of healthy, merit-based competition.
  • Defined Skill Tiers: Staff progress through clear skill tiers (e.g., Apprentice → Journeyman → Captain → Innovator) by excelling in challenges, creating a visible and motivating career ladder.

3) Staff Wellness & Retention Doctrine

Our approach to retention is simple: we invest in our team as a whole-person, creating an ecosystem of support that makes Alo Chaya a career destination. This is not a cost; it is our primary strategy for reducing staff turnover and ensuring the continuity of our service culture.

A. Investment in People: A Core Principle

  • Premium Accommodation & Meals: We will provide high-quality, safe, and comfortable staff accommodation and nutritious meals. This is a non-negotiable investment to maintain the high morale and energy required for a premium service environment.
  • Access to Wellness: All staff will have designated access to the Wellness Center facilities. By encouraging our team to embrace the 8 Dimensions of Wellness, we empower them to become authentic ambassadors of the Alo Chaya brand.

B. The Daycare Initiative: A Flagship CSR Project

The planned on-site daycare and education center is the flagship of our CSR and retention strategy. It is designed to create a profound, multi-generational bond with our employees and the local community.

  • Reduces Employee Stress: Providing a safe and enriching environment for their children is the single most powerful benefit we can offer our staff, dramatically increasing loyalty and reducing absenteeism.
  • Community Hub: The center will also offer educational resources to the broader Chandpur community, positioning Alo Chaya as a true community leader and a partner in local development.
  • Investor Value: This initiative proves a deep commitment to sustainable and ethical operations, a key factor for modern investors focused on ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) principles.

4) Leadership & Promotion Doctrine

Our leadership framework is built upon a synthesis of world-class management science to ensure that we identify, nurture, and empower leaders at every level. This doctrine governs how we manage teams and provides a clear, merit-based path for promotion.

A. Our Leadership Framework: The 5 Levels

We have integrated the leadership models of Jim Collins, John C. Maxwell, and Robert Kelley to define our culture:

  • The Goal (Collins' Level 5 Leadership): The founder and all senior leaders are expected to embody the "Level 5" ideal: a powerful mixture of deep personal humility and intense professional will. Our focus is on building the enduring greatness of Alo Chaya, not personal ego.
  • The Path (Maxwell's 5 Levels of Leadership): We provide a clear path for team members to grow from "Position" (a title) to "Pinnacle" (respect). Our "Shokugeki-style" challenges and mentorship are designed to help leaders earn genuine influence through their competence and character.
  • The Team (Kelley's Followership Model): We do not hire "staff"; we recruit "Exemplary Followers." We seek out and cultivate team members who are critical, independent thinkers and active, engaged partners in our mission.

B. Our Management & Promotion System

Promotions are earned through demonstrated competence and leadership maturity. We use two core models to guide daily management and evaluate readiness for promotion:

  • Situational Leadership (Hersey-Blanchard): Our mentors use the Skill/Will matrix to manage their teams. A new hire with high will but low skill receives direct guidance. A seasoned "Captain" with high will and high skill is delegated significant autonomy. This ensures every team member gets the precise support they need to succeed.
  • The Path to Promotion (Tannenbaum-Schmidt Continuum): A team member is ready for promotion when their leader consistently operates on the "right side" of the continuum with them—collaborating, delegating, and entrusting them with greater responsibility. This makes our promotion process an objective and transparent measure of demonstrated capability.
